# Work Experience

## Purpose

Work Experience documents professional engineering experience as a first-class Knowledge Node.

It is not a résumé.

It is not a job listing.

Instead, it documents engineering problems, technologies, lessons learned and how professional work influenced personal engineering growth.

Work Experience should naturally connect to Projects, Articles, Technologies, Timeline Events and future Knowledge Nodes.

---

# Goals

Work Experience pages should help visitors understand:

* what engineering domain the role belonged to
* what technologies were involved
* what was learned
* what projects or ideas originated from the experience
* how the experience fits into the broader engineering journey

The emphasis is engineering context rather than employment history.

---

# Metadata

Work Experience inherits the Knowledge Node architecture.

Additional recommended metadata includes:

* organization
* position
* location
* employmentType
* startDate
* endDate
* current
* technologies
* confidentiality
* externalLink

Existing metadata such as Status, Domains and Relationships should continue working unchanged.

---

# Confidentiality

Professional work may involve confidential information.

The theme should encourage documenting engineering lessons without exposing proprietary information.

Work Experience pages should emphasize:

* technologies
* engineering challenges
* methodologies
* lessons learned

Avoid requiring implementation details.

The architecture should naturally support both public and confidential work.

---

# Page Structure

Recommended layout:

Metadata

↓

Overview

↓

Role

↓

Engineering Context

↓

Technologies

↓

Projects and Related Knowledge

↓

Lessons Learned

↓

Related Articles

↓

Related Timeline Events

↓

Continue Exploring

Individual entries may omit sections when unnecessary.

---

# Technologies

Technologies used during employment should be presented similarly to Domains.

Examples:

* SystemVerilog
* UVM
* Git
* Linux
* Python
* KiCad

Technology presentation should remain consistent with the overall theme.

---

# Related Knowledge

Work Experience should naturally connect to:

* Projects
* Project Journals
* Blog Posts
* Technologies
* Books
* Timeline Events
* Future Knowledge Nodes

The Related Knowledge component introduced by the Knowledge Node architecture should be reused.

---

# Timeline Integration

Work Experience participates in the Timeline as a duration-based Knowledge Node.

The Timeline should visualize employment periods without requiring Work Experience pages to implement Timeline-specific rendering.

Timeline metadata remains optional.

---

# Presentation

The visual presentation should resemble engineering documentation.

Avoid corporate résumé aesthetics.

Prioritize:

* technical clarity
* chronology
* engineering context
* concise summaries

The page should feel similar to a project case study.

---

# Future Compatibility

Work Experience should remain compatible with:

* Timeline Index
* Search
* Knowledge Graph
* Related Knowledge
* Future Technology pages

No implementation should assume Work Experience is only viewed directly.

---

# Roadmap

## Phase 1

Introduce the Work Experience content type.

* archetype
* list layout
* single layout

Reuse the existing Knowledge Node architecture.

---

## Phase 2

Support organization metadata.

Support employment duration.

Support confidentiality metadata.

---

## Phase 3

Integrate Related Knowledge.

Connect Projects, Articles and Technologies.

---

## Phase 4

Integrate Timeline.

Employment should appear as duration-based entries.

---

## Phase 5

Polish presentation.

Improve metadata.

Improve navigation.

Improve engineering readability.

---

# Success Criteria

Work Experience is successful when:

* It feels like a natural extension of the Project pages.
* Engineering learning is emphasized over employment history.
* Visitors understand how professional work influenced later projects and learning.
* Related Knowledge encourages further exploration.
* The page remains useful even when engineering details must remain confidential.
