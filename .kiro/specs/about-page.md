# About Page Specification

## Purpose

The About page is not a résumé, portfolio or biography.

Its purpose is to introduce the engineer behind the website and provide the context needed to understand the rest of the knowledge base.

A visitor should leave the page understanding:

* Who I am.
* Why I became an engineer.
* Why this website exists.
* What motivates my work.
* What kinds of problems I enjoy solving.

Everything else on the website documents **what** I have done.

The About page explains **why**.

---

# User Story

As a visitor,

I want to understand the person behind the projects,

so that I understand the motivations, values and engineering philosophy driving the rest of the website.

The About page should make the rest of the website feel intentional rather than a collection of unrelated pages.

---

# Design Principles

The About page should be:

* Personal without becoming autobiographical.
* Professional without becoming corporate.
* Reflective without becoming self-promotional.
* Timeless rather than tied to a specific year.
* Focused on engineering thinking rather than achievements.

The page should feel like an introduction to an engineer, not an application for a job.

---

# Relationship to Other Sections

The About page intentionally avoids duplicating information found elsewhere.

Do NOT repeat:

* Project lists
* Work experience
* Education history
* Skill matrices
* Technology lists
* Certifications
* Chronological timelines

Those already have dedicated sections.

Instead, reference them when appropriate.

Example:

"I document my engineering journey publicly."

→ Link to Timeline.

"I enjoy building embedded systems."

→ Link to Projects.

---

# Recommended Structure

## Hero

Short introduction.

One or two paragraphs.

Should answer:

Who am I?

Why am I here?

What is this website?

---

## Why This Website Exists

Explain the purpose of maintaining a public engineering knowledge base.

Topics may include:

* documenting engineering decisions
* learning in public
* preserving knowledge
* long-term growth
* sharing ideas
* creating a personal reference

This section provides visitors with the mental model for navigating the rest of the website.

---

## Engineering Philosophy

Discuss principles rather than technologies.

Possible themes:

* curiosity
* systems thinking
* engineering over hype
* learning by building
* documenting decisions
* simplicity
* automation
* continuous improvement

Avoid discussing individual tools unless they illustrate a broader principle.

---

## Engineering Interests

Describe the kinds of engineering problems that are exciting.

Examples:

* Embedded Systems
* Robotics
* Digital Design
* Systems Programming
* Mechanical Design
* Engineering Tooling
* Developer Experience
* Knowledge Systems

Focus on interests rather than proficiency.

---

## Beyond Engineering

A short section showing the person behind the work.

Examples:

* books
* photography
* hiking
* design
* languages
* coffee
* travel

Keep this concise.

The page should remain engineering-focused.

---

## About This Website

Explain the website itself.

Topics may include:

* why it exists
* design philosophy
* knowledge architecture
* open source
* static site
* documentation approach

This is especially valuable for technically curious visitors.

---

## Contact

Provide clear ways to reach out.

Include:

* Email
* GitHub
* LinkedIn

Optionally mention the kinds of conversations you enjoy having.

Avoid generic statements such as:

"I am always open to new opportunities."

Prefer authentic invitations such as discussing embedded systems, digital design, open source, or engineering ideas.

---

# Writing Guidelines

The writing should feel thoughtful and genuine.

Prefer:

* stories over slogans
* principles over buzzwords
* examples over claims
* curiosity over certainty

Write in the first person.

Avoid exaggerated marketing language.

Avoid listing technologies simply to demonstrate breadth.

Show personality without losing professionalism.

---

# Success Criteria

After reading the About page, a visitor should understand:

* Who is Eduards?
* What kind of engineer is he becoming?
* Why does he document his work publicly?
* What values guide his engineering decisions?
* Why is this website different from a traditional portfolio?

The About page should create curiosity to continue exploring the rest of the website rather than attempting to summarize it.
