# Education

## Purpose

Education documents a significant educational environment as a Knowledge Node.

Examples include:

* University programmes
* Degrees
* High school
* Exchange programmes
* Long-form professional training

Education is not intended to document every individual course.

Instead, it provides the context in which engineering knowledge, projects and opportunities developed.

---

# Goals

An Education page should help visitors understand:

* what educational environment this represents
* what engineering opportunities it created
* what important work emerged during this period
* how it connects to other Knowledge Nodes

Education should communicate progression rather than qualifications.

---

# Metadata

Education inherits the shared Knowledge Node architecture.

Additional metadata may include:

* institution
* programme
* degree
* location
* startDate
* endDate
* current

The existing metadata system (status, domains, relationships, navigation, etc.) should continue to work unchanged.

---

# Educational Context

Education should describe the overall engineering environment.

Examples include:

* laboratory work
* access to makerspaces
* engineering culture
* collaboration
* multidisciplinary projects
* research opportunities

This section provides context rather than achievements.

---

# Student Activities

Education should support documenting significant student activities.

Examples:

* Makerspaces
* Student boards
* Technical committees
* Engineering societies
* Competition teams
* Student organizations
* Volunteer technical work

These activities should appear as part of the educational experience rather than as employment.

Each activity may include:

* role
* duration
* short description
* related Knowledge Nodes

---

# Thesis

Education should support highlighting a thesis or major capstone project.

The thesis should naturally connect to:

* Projects
* Work Experience
* Timeline
* Articles

The implementation should encourage reuse of existing Knowledge Nodes rather than duplicate information.

---

# Important Courses

Education should support highlighting selected courses.

This is intentionally not a transcript.

Only courses that meaningfully influenced later engineering work should be included.

Each course may optionally reference:

* Projects
* Articles
* Timeline Events
* Related Knowledge

---

# Related Knowledge

Education should naturally connect to other Knowledge Nodes.

Examples include:

* Projects
* Project Journals
* Work Experience
* Articles
* Books
* Workflows
* Timeline Events

The existing Related Knowledge component should be reused.

---

# Presentation

Education should resemble an engineering environment rather than an academic transcript.

Prioritize:

* context
* relationships
* engineering growth

Avoid:

* grade tables
* exhaustive course lists
* certificate collections
* résumé-style presentation

---

# Suggested Page Structure

Recommended order:

Metadata

↓

Overview

↓

Educational Context

↓

Student Activities

↓

Thesis

↓

Important Courses

↓

Related Knowledge

↓

Continue Exploring

Individual pages may omit sections when they are not applicable.

---

# Timeline Integration

Education should participate in the Timeline as a duration-based Knowledge Node.

Timeline rendering should remain independent from Education pages.

Education exposes metadata.

Timeline consumes it.

---

# Future Compatibility

The Education architecture should remain compatible with future Knowledge Nodes including:

* Courses
* Certifications
* Research
* Publications
* Talks

The implementation should not assume these exist today.

---

# Roadmap

## Phase 1

Introduce the Education content type.

Create:

* archetype
* list layout
* single layout

Reuse the existing Knowledge Node architecture.

---

## Phase 2

Support student activities.

Support optional role metadata.

Support related Knowledge Nodes.

---

## Phase 3

Support thesis presentation.

Integrate with existing Projects and Work Experience.

---

## Phase 4

Support curated Important Courses.

Allow optional relationships to Projects, Articles and Timeline Events.

---

## Phase 5

Refine presentation.

Improve navigation.

Improve readability.

Ensure consistency with the Maker Log design language.

---

# Success Criteria

The Education page is successful when visitors understand:

* the engineering environment in which learning occurred
* the opportunities that environment created
* how projects, student activities and professional work emerged from it
* how education connects to the broader engineering journey

Education should feel like the story of an engineering ecosystem rather than a record of academic achievements.
