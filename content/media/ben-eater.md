## Why I Watch This

Ben Eater builds complex systems from first principles on a breadboard. His 8-bit computer series is one of the best introductions to computer architecture that exists — no abstraction, just gates and wires.

## Key Ideas

- Building a CPU from NAND gates makes architecture intuition physical
- Networking protocols become obvious when you see the signals on a scope
- Patience in debugging hardware is a transferable engineering skill

## Influence on Engineering

Reinforced the value of understanding systems at every level of abstraction. Directly motivated my interest in digital design and eventually RTL/ASIC work.
